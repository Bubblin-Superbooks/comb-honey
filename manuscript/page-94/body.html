Control Of Natural Swarms.

Natural swarms may be managed (1 by allowing them to cluster naturally, then hiving them in the ordinary manner; (2 by the clipped queen method; (3) by the use of queen traps ( by use of the swarm catcher.) )

This is simply a wire-cloth cage large enough to be set over the hive or be fitted over the entrance. If the attendant is provided with a number of these catchers he can avoid the usual confusion ordinarily occurring when several swarms issue at about the same time.
